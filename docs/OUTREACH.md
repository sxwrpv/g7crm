## 1. Daily ops drumbeat

### Day rhythm

| Time | Block |
|---|---|
| 8:30am | Coffee. Open G7CRM. Pull today's call list (~30-50 leads). |
| 9:00–11:00 | **Outbound dials.** 30+ minimum. Most won't pick up — that's normal. Voicemail + SMS for every miss. |
| 11:00–12:00 | Reply window. Answer texts/emails from morning. |
| 12:00–14:00 | Lunch + CRM admin. Send Friday digests (Tuesdays). |
| 14:00–17:00 | Onboarding work for paying clients. If none → more dials. |
| 17:00–18:30 | **Second outbound block.** Trades often pick up after the work day ends. |
| 18:30 | Day's end. Log every call. Set tomorrow's list. |

### Dial volume by week

| Week | Daily target | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 50 dials | Get rejection out of your system fast |
| 2 | 30 dials | More time on follow-ups from week 1 |
| 3+ | 20-30 dials | Pipeline maintaining itself |

### Calling days

- **Tue · Wed · Thu** — best. Trades working, picking up.
- **Mon morning** — skip. Starting the week.
- **Fri** — OK for follow-ups, not first calls.

### Expected funnel (rough; expect worse in week 1)

```
50 dials  →  5-7 connects  →  2-3 audit calls booked  →  1 signed
```

So **50 dials ≈ 1 paying client per day on a good day.**

## 2. Cold call script

### Opening (15 seconds — pattern interrupt)

> *"Hi, [first name], is that you?"*
>
> *[wait — let them confirm]*
>
> *"This is Arsen from G7 Systems in Dublin. I know it's a cold call — give me 30 seconds and I'll tell you why you might actually want to hear it?"*

Likely responses:
- *"Go on"* → continue
- *"What's it about?"* → continue (good sign, curiosity)
- *"Not interested"* → *"Fair enough. Is it OK if I send you a one-line text instead so you can decide later?"* → if yes, send the SMS template

### Problem question (the wedge — get them admitting the leak)

> *"Quick question — when you're on a job and the phone rings, what happens to that call?"*

Listen. Common answers + your follow-up:
- **"Goes to voicemail"** → *"And do most of those people call back? Or do they ring the next [plumber/electrician]?"*
- **"My wife / partner takes them"** → *"How does that work when she's busy too? Weekends, evenings?"*
- **"I call them back later"** → *"What's the typical gap — 30 minutes, couple hours?"*
- **"I have an answering service"** → *"How much is that running you a month? And do they actually book the job, or just take the message?"*

The point: **get them saying out loud that leads are leaking.** Not arguing.

### Micro-pitch (30 sec — after they've admitted the leak)

> *"OK, so what we do at G7 Systems — we run the system that catches every missed call, every form, every WhatsApp message, and replies to the customer within 5 minutes. Day, night, weekend. Sounds like what you need.*
>
> *We also build the website if yours is a bit dated.*
>
> *I'd want to do a 15-minute call with you, no pitch — just look at where your leads are slipping and write up a 1-page action list. Free. If you want us to fix it after, we charge €290 a month. First month is free."*
>
> *"Does that sound like 15 minutes worth taking?"*

### Close

- **Yes** → *"Great. What's better for you, Tuesday or Thursday morning?"* (give two options — avoid open question)
- **Maybe** → *"OK, I'll text you the one-line summary and the form. No pressure, just read it on the toilet later."*
- **No** → *"Fair enough. Can I send you a one-line text so you have it on file in case anything changes?"*

### If they ask "where did you get my number?"

> *"Honest answer: Google. You're listed as a [plumber/electrician] in Dublin and your number's on your site. Same way your customers find you."*

### Tone notes

- **Talk slow.** Tradies hate fast-talking sales.
- **Don't say "AI", "automation", "platform".** Say "system", "we", "us".
- **Don't push.** Be the friendly neighbour, not a vendor.
- **Be Dublin-specific.** "We only cover Dublin" reinforces local trust.
- **Smile while you talk.** They hear it.

## 3. Voicemail + SMS templates

### Voicemail (20 seconds, when no answer)

> *"Hi [first name], this is Arsen from G7 Systems in Dublin. Quick reason for the call — I help [plumbers / electricians / heating engineers] in Dublin stop losing leads to missed calls and late replies. We're free for the first month. I'll send you a one-line text so you have it on hand. Cheers, talk soon."*

### SMS — within 5 minutes of the voicemail

> Hey [first name] — Arsen from G7 Systems. Just left a voicemail. Quick context: we help Dublin trades stop losing leads to missed calls. Reply in 5 min, 24/7, no DIY software. First month free. Worth a 15-min call this week? Here's a one-pager: g7systems.xyz

### SMS — 24 hours later (if no reply)

> Hey [first name], Arsen here. Followed up from yesterday. No worries if it's not the right time. If you ever want a look at what's leaking, the audit's free: g7systems.xyz/#audit. Talk soon.

### SMS — 7 days later (one last polite touch, then close the file)

> Hey [first name] — closing my notes on this. If anything changes with the call/lead handling, here's where to find us: g7systems.xyz. All the best.

## 4. Post-call email template

Use when they say *"send me an email and I'll think about it"*.

**Subject:** G7 Systems — the 15-min audit we talked about

> Hi [first name],
>
> Cheers for the call. As promised, here's where to book the free 15-min audit:
>
> https://g7systems.xyz/#audit
>
> What you get: a 1-page action list showing where your leads are slipping, what each leak is costing you per week, and what to do about it. No pitch unless you ask.
>
> If now isn't the right time, no harm done. Keep me in mind.
>
> — Arsen
> G7 Systems · Dublin
> hello@g7systems.xyz

Keep it short. No links to social, no signature image, no "best regards". One link, one promise, one sign-off.

## 5. Top 5 objections + responses

### "We have someone already / we have a girl in the office"

> *"What's her response time looking like? What we do is reply in 5 minutes even at 2am. She's probably brilliant during the day, but unless she's at her desk on a Saturday morning, you're still losing weekend leads. Worth a 15-min look?"*

### "We're too busy"

> *"That's actually the perfect reason to take a 15-min look. The busier you are, the more calls you're missing. If you don't have time for the audit, you definitely don't have time for the leaks."*

### "We're too small for that"

> *"How many calls do you miss a week? Even 2-3 missed = €1,000+ of work walking away. The system pays for itself with one job a month. And the first month is free anyway, so you literally can't lose money trying."*

### "How is this different from [Tradify / ServiceM8 / Jobber]"

> *"Software is something you DIY. We do it for you. You don't log into anything. You just keep doing your work and the leads come to you already qualified. We're an agency, not a SaaS."*

### "Send me an email and I'll think about it"

> *"Of course. Quick one before I do — would you prefer to look at this in a 15-minute call where I walk you through it, or do you actually like getting random sales emails? Up to you. I'll happily send either."*

## 7. How to find more prospects (corrected methodology)

### Where the right prospects actually live

The web-search "best plumbers Dublin" trick **does not work** — it returns the businesses that ranked top, which means they're competent at digital marketing already. Use these instead:

1. **goldenpages.ie** (best source) — many Irish trades list there with just a phone, no website link. Browse `goldenpages.ie/q/business/advanced/where/Dublin/what/Handyman-Services/`, then swap "Handyman-Services" for "Plumbers", "Electricians", etc. **Filter visually for listings with no website icon.** Handyman directory has ~80% no-website listings — goldmine.
2. **Google Maps with the "no website" filter** — search "plumber Dublin", open the list view, look for listings that don't have a website link. These are typically the smaller operators.
3. **Facebook Marketplace + Adverts.ie** — sole traders post "plumbing/electrical/handyman services Dublin available" with mobile only. Zero digital infrastructure.
4. **Van logos on Dublin streets** — literally write down the phone numbers painted on the side of work vans parked outside houses. The smaller they are, the better the target.

### Tooled methodology (€5-50)

- **Outscraper Google Maps** ([outscraper.com](https://outscraper.com)) — €0.001/result, CSV export, includes website (or lack of). Filter the CSV for blank-website rows.
- **Apify Google Maps Scraper** — €0.50 per 100 results.

### Trades hierarchy (by likelihood of finding no-website prospects)

| Trade | % without website on goldenpages |
|---|---|
| **Handyman** | ~80% — best source |
| **Painting/Decorating** | ~50% |
| **Cleaning** | ~40% |
| **Gardening** | ~40% |
| **Carpentry** | ~30% |
| **Plumbing** | ~5% — most have websites |
| **Electrical** | ~5% — RECI/Safe Electric pushes them online |

**Counter-intuitive insight:** handymen are the goldmine because they're unlicensed → smaller → less digital sophistication. Plumbers and electricians need licenses (RGI/RECI), which correlates with running an actual business with a website. Lead with handymen for the first batch of wins.

### Tooled methodology (€10-50, ~50 prospects in 10 min)

- **Outscraper Google Maps** ([outscraper.com](https://outscraper.com)) — €0.001 per result, exports CSV with name/phone/website/rating. ~€5 for 5,000 results.
- **Apify Google Maps Scraper** — €0.50 per 100 results.

### What to look for (signals of weak digital presence)

| Signal | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| No website OR a Wix/SquareSpace from before 2020 | Easy upgrade sell |
| Only one phone number, no contact form | Definitely missing leads |
| Mon-Fri 9-5 hours posted | Evening/weekend calls go cold |
| <50 Google reviews | Less established, more open to help |
| Single-trade focus (only plumbing, not "plumbing + electrical + heating") | Smaller operator, decision-maker is the owner |
| Listed on goldenpages with no website link | Old-school operator |

### What to AVOID (these have already solved this)

- 1,000+ reviews
- 20+ staff visible on "About" page
- Already uses ServiceTitan / Jobber / Tradify branded materials
- Multiple branches / multiple locations
- Recently won industry awards

## 8. CRM logging

Every call. No exceptions. After 100 calls you'll have actual data on which trade vertical responds best.

### Required fields per call

```
record_type      = lead
source_channel   = cold-call
last_contacted_at = now()
next_action      = <specific: "Follow up Thu / sent SMS / wants audit Tue 10am / not interested - close">
notes            = <2-line summary: what they said, what their objection was>
lead_temperature = cold | warm | hot
```

### Status progression

| After call result | Set `next_action` to |
|---|---|
| Booked audit | `"Audit on <day> <time>"`; flip `record_type=prospect` |
| Said send email | `"Sent follow-up email <date>; ping again in 5 days"` |
| Voicemail only | `"Sent SMS; check Friday for reply"` |
| Polite no | `"Send SMS in 7d; close if no response"` |
| Aggressive no | `"Close — do not contact"`; flip `record_type=former-client` |

### Friday review

At 5pm Friday, run this query in the dashboard / Supabase SQL editor:

```sql
select niche, count(*) filter (where lead_temperature='hot') as hot,
                  count(*) filter (where lead_temperature='warm') as warm,
                  count(*) as total
from customers
where source_channel = 'cold-call'
  and last_contacted_at > now() - interval '7 days'
group by niche
order by hot desc, warm desc;
```

This is how you find which trade vertical is responding. Double down on the top one.
